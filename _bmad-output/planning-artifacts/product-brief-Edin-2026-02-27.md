# Product Brief: Edin

## Executive Summary

Edin is a curated contributor platform designed to organize, evaluate, and reward collaborative development within the Rose decentralized finance ecosystem. Named after the Sumerian word for a fertile plain, Edin embodies the vision of a cultivated garden where diverse, high-caliber contributors come together to build next-generation financial infrastructure — and are fairly recognized for it.

The platform addresses a fundamental market failure: open-source contributors produce enormous value but receive almost no structured compensation, while existing incentivization platforms rely on subjective evaluation and short-term bounties that reward activity over impact. Edin's distinctive approach combines three innovations no competitor has fused together: an AI-powered Evaluation Engine that objectively assesses contribution quality across multiple domains (code, financial engineering, governance, impact), a multi-scale reward system distributing blockchain-based tokens across seven temporal horizons using scaling-law mathematics derived from Rose's own financial research, and a curated admission model that ensures community quality through selectivity rather than open access.

Edin is not a productivity suite — it is an integration-first platform that connects to the tools contributors already use (GitHub, Google Workspace, Slack), analyzes their output, and translates quality and impact into fair rewards. Contributors keep their workflows; Edin provides the intelligence layer.

Built within the IOUR Foundation (Belgian non-profit), Edin operates at the intersection of three converging forces: AI transformation of work, open-source sustainability crisis, and blockchain economics maturity. The recommended strategy is to build for Rose first with modular architecture, validate the evaluation methodology with a founding community of 20-30 contributors, then offer the methodology to other ecosystems as a service — creating an independent sustainability path beyond the Rose ecosystem.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

The open-source development model is broken at its economic core. Over 100 million developers contribute to open-source projects globally, yet the vast majority receive zero financial compensation for their work. The platforms that exist to address this — Gitcoin, Coordinape, SourceCred, GitHub Sponsors — suffer from three structural weaknesses:

1. **Subjective evaluation.** Contribution quality is assessed through peer voting, community sentiment, or maintainer discretion — methods that are inherently political, inconsistent, and unscalable. Contributors frequently feel that recognition is unfair.

2. **Short-term economics.** The dominant model is task-based bounties: do work, get paid, relationship ends. There is no mechanism to reward sustained, long-term engagement or to recognize the compounding impact of foundational contributions.

3. **Code-centrism.** Non-code contributions — documentation, financial modeling, governance design, community building, impact assessment — are systematically undervalued or invisible, even when they are essential to a project's success.

For the Rose ecosystem specifically, there is an additional challenge: building a next-generation decentralized financial infrastructure requires deep expertise across technology, financial engineering, impact analysis, and governance — four domains that must work in concert. No existing platform organizes multi-domain contribution at this level of sophistication.

### Problem Impact

- **Contributor burnout and attrition.** High-quality contributors leave open-source projects when their work goes unrecognized, reducing development velocity and institutional knowledge.
- **Quality degradation.** Without structured evaluation, projects cannot distinguish high-impact contributions from low-value ones, leading to noise, technical debt, and misallocated attention.
- **Talent scarcity.** Skilled professionals — especially outside software development — have no clear path to contribute to decentralized projects, even when they possess essential expertise. Their time goes to traditional employment or consulting instead.
- **Community fragility.** Without fair incentive structures, contributor communities depend entirely on intrinsic motivation, which is powerful but insufficient for sustained, large-scale collaboration.
- **Innovation bottleneck.** Rose's ambitious financial infrastructure cannot be built by a small core team alone. Without a scalable, incentivized contributor model, the project's execution capacity is capped by its payroll.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

| Platform                              | What It Does                                  | Where It Fails                                                                                         |
| ------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Gitcoin**                           | Community-voted grant rounds and bounties     | Low evaluation sophistication (popularity contest), short-term only, no sustained engagement incentive |
| **Coordinape**                        | Peer-based epoch rewards within small teams   | Subjective circle voting, struggles at scale, no quality assessment beyond social consensus            |
| **SourceCred**                        | Algorithmic graph-based contribution scoring  | Innovative but limited adoption, no multi-domain support, no blockchain reward integration             |
| **Colony**                            | Task-based evaluation with reputation staking | Structurally complex, medium-term rewards, no scaling-law compounding                                  |
| **GitHub Sponsors / Open Collective** | Donation-based funding                        | No structured evaluation whatsoever, irregular and unpredictable, dependent on individual donors       |

The strategic white space is clear: **no platform combines AI-powered objective evaluation with multi-temporal compounding rewards and a curated community model.** This intersection is entirely unoccupied.

### Proposed Solution

Edin is an integration-first contributor platform organized around five functional layers:

1. **Integration Layer** — Connectors to external tools (GitHub, Google Workspace, Slack, project management) that ingest contributor outputs without imposing workflow changes. Contributors work with the tools they already know; Edin analyzes what they produce.

2. **Web Portal** — Public-facing website and contributor dashboard for community interaction, onboarding, profiles, activity feeds, and project showcase.

3. **AI-Powered Evaluation Engine** — Automated assessment of contribution quality across multiple domains: code quality analysis, documentation evaluation, collaboration scoring, and goal alignment assessment. Evaluations are transparent — contributors see how their work was scored and can understand the criteria.

4. **Multi-Scale Reward System** — Token-based rewards distributed across seven temporal horizons (hourly to yearly) using a scaling-law methodology where longer-term engagement yields disproportionately greater rewards. Smart contracts on L2 blockchain ensure trustless, transparent distribution.

5. **Governance Layer** — Curated admission process, role management across four strategic categories (Technology, Finance, Impact, Governance), and progressive decentralization toward community-driven decision-making.

The platform follows a phased approach: Foundation (Q1 2026) → MVP (Q2 2026) → Intelligence (Q3-Q4 2026) → Scale (2027+), with a recommended strategy of validating the methodology with Rose first, then offering it as a service to other ecosystems.

### Key Differentiators

1. **AI-Objective Evaluation** — While every competitor relies on human judgment (peer voting, maintainer discretion), Edin uses AI to provide objective, scalable, transparent quality assessment. This eliminates the most corrosive force in open-source communities: the perception that recognition is unfair.

2. **Scaling-Law Compounding Rewards** — The entire market operates on single-task bounties. Edin's multi-temporal reward system transforms contributors from contractors into stakeholders: the longer you stay, the more disproportionately you earn. This is directly inspired by the same mathematical scaling laws powering Rose's Alpha Engine — a genuine intellectual moat.

3. **Integration-First, Not Tool-Replacement** — Most platforms try to become the workspace. Edin does the opposite: it connects to existing tools and focuses exclusively on evaluation and reward. Zero adoption friction. Zero workflow disruption.

4. **Curated Community as Network Effect** — Open access is the industry default. Edin's admission-based model creates a selectivity flywheel: high-quality members attract high-quality applicants, which justifies the selectivity, which raises the signal value of membership.

5. **Multi-Domain Equality by Design** — Four structural pillars (Technology, Finance, Impact, Governance) ensure that non-code contributions are architecturally first-class citizens, not afterthoughts. Financial engineers, governance designers, and impact analysts have the same visibility, contribution pathways, and reward mechanisms as developers.

6. **Human Empowerment in an AI World** — As AI automates routine development, Edin explicitly creates space for uniquely human capabilities: mentoring, governance, creative problem-solving, and leadership. This narrative is entirely uncontested in the Web3 contributor space.

## Success Metrics

**North Star Metric:**

> **Active contributor retention rate at 90 days: target >40% quarter-over-quarter**
>
> This single metric captures whether Edin delivers on its core promise: a community worth staying in. If contributors stay, the evaluation works, the rewards are motivating, and the community has quality. If they leave, nothing else matters.

### User Success Metrics

| Metric                                | What It Measures                                               | Target                                                                                    | How We Know It's Working                                                               |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Time-to-first-contribution**        | How quickly new contributors become active                     | <72 hours for >80% of admitted contributors                                               | The 72-Hour Ignition onboarding design compresses the gap between admission and action |
| **Multi-domain contribution balance** | Whether non-developer contributors feel equal                  | At least 1 active contributor per domain (Tech, Finance, Impact, Governance) at all times | The four-pillar structure works — Amir, Sofia, and Yuki are as engaged as Lena         |
| **Belonging score**                   | Emotional experience of community membership                   | Average >=4/5 on "How valued do you feel?" in contributor surveys                         | Contributors feel chosen and respected, not processed and filed                        |
| **Peer feedback satisfaction**        | Whether feedback is useful before AI evaluation exists         | >60% rate peer feedback as "useful" or "very useful"                                      | The bridge mechanism holds until the Evaluation Engine is online                       |
| **Referral willingness**              | Whether contributors are proud enough to recruit peers         | >70% would invite someone they respect                                                    | The ultimate quality signal — people only refer to things they're proud of             |
| **AI evaluation trust score**         | Whether contributors perceive AI evaluation as fair (Phase 2+) | >70% rate evaluation as "fair" or "very fair"                                             | The Evaluation Engine is credible and contributors don't feel gamed                    |
| **Reward comprehension rate**         | Whether contributors understand the scaling-law model          | >80% can explain the model when asked                                                     | The reward system motivates because it's understood, not just theoretically elegant    |

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

**The MVP must answer one question: can we attract, activate, and retain founding contributors with enough tooling to validate the core hypotheses — before building the full platform?**

The MVP aligns with Phase 1 (Foundation + early MVP) of the roadmap. It delivers the minimum needed to run "The First Cohort" prototype from the design thinking session, plus the first technology building blocks.

**1. Web Portal — Contributor Dashboard (Basic)**

- Public-facing project page: mission, vision, Founding Circle roster, domain manifestos
- Contributor profiles: name, bio, domain, contribution history, role
- Activity feed: real-time stream of contributions across all domains (the "Contribution Wall")
- Application workflow: contribution-based admission with domain-specific micro-tasks
- Basic contributor dashboard: personal view of contributions submitted and feedback received

**2. GitHub Integration (Primary Integration)**

- Repository connector ingesting commit history, pull requests, reviews, and CI/CD outputs
- Contribution attribution tracking — who did what, when, and in what context
- Automated ingestion pipeline normalizing GitHub artifacts for the Evaluation Engine

**3. AI Evaluation Engine — Prototype**

- Code quality analysis: automated assessment of complexity, maintainability, test coverage, and standards adherence from ingested GitHub data
- Documentation evaluation: completeness and clarity scoring for written contributions
- Evaluation transparency: contributors see how their work was scored with explainable criteria
- Target: >70% agreement with human expert assessment
- Scope limited to GitHub-ingested artifacts (code + docs). Broader multi-source evaluation deferred to Phase 2

**4. Structured Peer Feedback System**

- Lightweight feedback rubric (5-7 questions) for contributions not yet covered by AI evaluation
- Assignment mechanism: each meaningful contribution receives at least one peer review
- Feedback visible to the contributor on their dashboard
- Bridge mechanism until AI evaluation covers all contribution types

**5. Community Structure — Four Pillars**

- Four domain working groups (Technology, Finance, Impact, Governance) with dedicated communication channels
- Curated contribution menu: 15-20+ tasks across all domains, tagged by difficulty and effort, regularly refreshed
- Buddy system: each new contributor paired with an existing contributor for 30 days
- Basic meeting infrastructure for working group sessions

**6. Admission & Onboarding — 72-Hour Ignition**

- Contribution-based application flow (domain-specific micro-task)
- Admission review process (manual, by existing contributors)
- Welcome call coordination (peer welcome committee)
- Curated first-task assignment during onboarding
- Founding Contributor designation with defined privileges

### Out of Scope for MVP

| Feature                                                         | Why It's Deferred                                                                                                                                                                  | Target Phase |
| --------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------ |
| **Blockchain token rewards**                                    | Token economics design, MiCA classification, and L2 infrastructure selection must be completed first. Premature token launch could create regulatory and trust risks               | Phase 2      |
| **Multi-source integration** (Google Workspace, Slack, Discord) | GitHub integration alone provides sufficient contribution data for Phase 1 validation. Additional integrations add maintenance burden for a <10 person team                        | Phase 2      |
| **Full multi-temporal reward distribution** (7 scales)          | Start with simpler recognition mechanisms (peer feedback, contribution wall, Founding Contributor privileges). Validate community viability before layering complex reward systems | Phase 2-3    |
| **DAO governance / on-chain voting**                            | Progressive decentralization is a Phase 3 goal. Phase 1 governance is direct, human, and deliberate through working group discussions and founding contributor input               | Phase 3      |
| **Contributor Portable Reputation**                             | Requires a mature evaluation engine and sufficient contribution data to be meaningful. Premature portability adds complexity without value                                         | Phase 3      |
| **AI Agent contributor class**                                  | Fascinating but not essential for founding community validation. Humans first, AI agents later                                                                                     | Phase 3      |
| **Evaluation-as-a-Service / external licensing**                | The methodology must be validated internally before offering externally. External licensing is the Option B sustainability play — it follows proof-of-concept, not precedes it     | Phase 3      |
| **Advanced analytics and forecasting**                          | Contribution pattern analysis, skill gap detection, and development recommendations require historical data that doesn't exist yet                                                 | Phase 2-3    |
| **Staking / vesting mechanisms**                                | Dependent on token economics design and regulatory classification                                                                                                                  | Phase 2-3    |
